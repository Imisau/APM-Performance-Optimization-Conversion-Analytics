# üîπ Page 1: Executive APM Funnel & KPI Overview

**Insight:** Revenue Leakage Occurs After Authorization

## What the data shows:
-	Strong authorization performance
-	Noticeable drop between authorized and completed transactions
-	Stable transaction values

## Interpretation:
Authorization is not the limiting factor. Revenue loss occurs after customer commitment, suggesting issues such as confirmation failures, timeout risks, or redirect friction.
Implication for APM Strategy:
This represents the fastest conversion uplift opportunity, as it does not require new markets or payment methods.
________________________________________

# üîπ Page 2: Payment Method Performance & Adoption

**Insight:** Alternative Payment Methods Perform on Par with Cards

## What the data shows:
-	Wallets, USSD, and bank-based APMs show authorization rates comparable to cards
-	Completion rates follow a uniform pattern across all methods
-	Transaction values are consistent regardless of payment type

## Interpretation:
APMs are not underperforming due to lack of customer trust or low usage quality. Performance gaps are systemic and shared across methods.
Implication for APM Strategy:
This validates continued investment in local APMs, especially in low-card-penetration markets.
________________________________________

# üîπ Page 3: Market & Failure Pattern Analysis

**Insight:** Markets Are Ready, Execution Quality Varies

## What the data shows:
-	Some markets exhibit high authorization but lower completion
-	Failures cluster around technical and process-related issues
-	No single market or payment method dominates failures

## Interpretation:
Markets are structurally ready for APM usage, but conversion outcomes depend on local execution quality, partner performance, and flow reliability.
Implication for APM Strategy:
Lower-performing markets should be treated as optimization opportunities, not deprioritized regions.
